What is Fibre and FTTH?

1.Fibre is simply the shortened term for Fibre optic technology.

2.FTTH is shorthand for Fibre to the Home. This means a fibre optic strand is linked directly to your home.

Can I get Fibre?

Please check on the coverage map via the following link to see if your area has Fibre coverage from any Fibre network provider. Coverage map

How Fibre is different from DSL?

DSL lines require copper wire infrastructure for the transference of data whereas fibre optics transfer data through pulses of light over fibre optic cables. The result is a much more enhanced internet experience, faster speeds, almost simultaneous upload and download times, scalability, reliability and enhanced internet security. Fibre optics are not affected by weather and are not susceptible to interruptions resulting from copper wire theft.

What is a Fibre Network and Fibre ISP?

Network: Fibre networks (Vumatel, OpenServe, Frogfoot etc) are the infrastructure providers that owns the fibre in the ground around your house.

ISP: Fibre ISPs are the internet service providers that provide internet services on top of the fibre networks.

If your internet is slow then you can try these solutions:

1. Try using different websites. A particular website might be slow, because that website is experiencing a lot of traffic at once.

2. If you have multiple online apps running in the background, close them.

3. Turn your Wi-Fi router off. Then turn your CPE or ONT (the fibre device mounted to your wall) off. Wait for 2 minutes. Turn the CPE or ONT back on. Then turn the Wi-Fi router back on.

4. If you are getting the right speeds while connected to the CPE or ONT device, then this means that your fibre line is fine.

5. Your speed issues may therefore sit with the ISP. Please log a ticket with us on our contact page and we can help you get in contact with your ISP.

Why does my Wi-Fi say “no internet access”?

Check with your service provider if you paid for your fibre service that month.

There might be a large amount of people on your current network, and it is unable to accommodate them all.

Why does my internet connection keep disconnecting?

There are many reasons why your internet connection may keep dropping:


1. Some Internet Service Providers (ISPs) include Fair Usage Policies in their internet packages. If you have not followed this Fair Usage Policy, your line may be dropped at intervals or cut off completely.

2. If you cannot reach a particular website, it could be that the website is unavailable at that time.

3. Check that you have not exceeded your cap and that you have followed your ISPs Fair Usage Policy. Capped products tend to experience issues when nearing their limit.


These following steps might be able to help if you are still disconnctiong:


1. Turn your Wi-Fi router off. Then turn your CPE or ONT (the fibre device mounted to your wall) off. Wait for 2 minutes. Turn the CPE or ONT back on. Then turn the Wi-Fi router back on.

2. Disable your anti-virus and firewall. Does this improve your connectivity? If so, your anti-virus and firewall may be interfering with your internet connection. Be sure to turn these back on after testing, but if your issues can be located here, you may need to look into trying a new firewall or anti-virus software.

3. If you still keep disconnecting, please transition to our contact page to log a ticket with your ISP. Once you have logged the fault with your ISP, they will investigate the issue further. If they find that the issue cannot be resolved at their layer, they will escalate the problem for further investigation.

Do I need a telephone line to use fibre optics?

No. You do not need any telephone lines to use fibre as the connection carries data via light across glass cables, not copper wire cables.

Can I use my current DSL router?

Fibre works on a completely different system to DSL; you can't necessarily use the same router.

However, it's possible to use your existing Wi-Fi enabled router to transfer the fibre connection via Wi-Fi.

This all depends on the existing router you have and how the provider installs the fibre.

Does the fibre provider install all the devices?

Not all ISP's will install the necessary routers and, if they do so, many will charge an extra cost for this work as it doesn’t come standard

It's best not to attempt the installation yourself.

Hardware troubleshoot

If you have tried all the support solutions and still have troubles then there might be a hardware issue . If the following solutions does not help and you think there might be another issue please head over to our contact page to contact your service provider for further assistance.

Hardware question 1:How old is your Wi-Fi router?

Outdated hardware, especially with computers and mobile devices, is a well-known limiting factor. If you have a 10 Mbps internet line but your old Wi-Fi router has a maximum output of 5 Mbps, you’ll only ever get up to half of what you’re paying for. Also, even if your router is not outdated, the firmware may need an occasional update.

Hardware question 2 :Have you reset your router from time to time?

Rebooting your router by switching it off and waiting for 30 seconds before turning it on again often solves connectivity issues, which usually arise from minor software faults in routers that are left on for extended periods.

Hardware question 3 Hoave you moved disruptive appliances away from your router?

Appliances such as microwaves, baby monitors, cordless phones, and even other routers can broadcast signals that actively congest your Wi-Fi spectrum if you operate on the 2.4Ghz frequency. If you have any of these appliances positioned nearby your router, try moving them away and see if there’s an improvement. Alternatively, if you’re worried that a neighbor’s Wi-Fi is disrupting your signal, you can try following this guide from Tech Advisor on changing your router channel.

Hardware question 4 Did you try to check the plugs and socket plug?.

Plugs and socket plugs might be the problem. Try switching socket plugs if the wifi lights still don't go on there might be another hardware issue contact your service provider to prevent payments from happening until the issue is resolved. Contact the brand of the router and the technicians will check it out

Hardware question 5 Do you Understand the lights on your router?

The power light indicates the status of power to the CPE device. The power light should be on and stable. If the power light is off, please ensure that the device is plugged in and switched on at the wall. If the light is flashing, switch the device off for 5 minutes and then turn it back on. This reboots the system. If the light continues to flash, this will require intervention. Please log a ticket with your ISP.

Hardware question 6 Have you considered using wired connections?

You will find that video streaming is data-intensive and may require more bandwidth, causing other services and devices to lag. As more users connect to the Wi-Fi, you may notice periodic lag depending on the number of Wi-Fi participants and their usage requirements. A wired ethernet cable connection is always more stable than a Wi-Fi signal from the same router. For bandwidth-intensive activities like gaming and high-definition streaming, consider using a wired connection instead. Also, by making use of a cable you will be redirecting traffic from the Wi-Fi router and reducing some of the Wi-Fi traffic.

Hardware question 7 When the power goes out

With load shedding in full swing, it’s worth noting that it may impact your provider’s ability to keep you connected to the web. Your service may be disrupted by a power break on either side of the service provider. It’s highly advisable to have surge protection fitted on your DB board to protect all your sensitive equipment like laptops, electronic appliances, modems and routers from power surges when the power comes back on. Consider installing an Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS), a device that allows a computer and routers connected to it to keep running for long enough to save any work and safely power down your devices when the primary power source is lost. It also provides protection from power surges.
